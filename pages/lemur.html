Lemur

Maybe you live in a pet-free home. If so, then shame on you! But when your circumstances change then, naturally, you’ll want a very special pet.

This cute fellow is about the same size as a common cat or a small yappy dog, and you'll be the talk of the neighbourhood if you adopt one.


Discover more about this magical creature by answering these four questions:

A1. It’s commonly called an aye-aye.

It’s not clear how the aye-aye got its name. It is possible that it originated with the local people who use "heh heh" to refer to "He-Who-Must-Not-Be-Named" - A feared magical creature.

A3. If you guessed at the obvious answer, that it uses it’s finger to hunt for food, then you guessed right.

However, it’s recently been discovered that it also uses the finger to pick its nose!

The discovery has triggered a wave of speculation about why so many of us primates, like the aye-aye, should get the urge to pick our nose.


Q4. Why do you pick your nose?

Check my answer

Admit it! We all do it. Teenagers pick their noses on average four times a day.

These days we tend to think of nose-picking as a bad habit. The behaviour is discouraged and not talked about in polite company.

However, the behaviour is also widespread among us primates, so it is likely that there is some evolutionary advantage to be gained.

Perhaps we should *encourage* everyone to pick their nose and eat it!
